Dr. Francis Crick

James Watson, Francis Crick and Maclyn McCarty at Cambridge University

Francis Harry Compton Crick was a British molecular biologist, biophysicist, and neuroscientist.

In 1953, he co-authored with James Watson the academic paper proposing the double helix structure of the DNA molecule. Together with Watson and Maurice Wilkins, he was jointly awarded the 1962 Nobel Prize in Medicine.
